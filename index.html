About me

When I was in seventh grade my father somehow borrowed an Apple II+ computer. It came with 16k of memory (count the bytes, folks), a 4" black and white portable tv monitor and a cassete tape drive for storage. We tinkered around for a while with it. Then I locked myself into the basement for two weeks and emerged a BASIC programmer. That's how all this started.

In high school I experienced a crisis. College was directly ahead of me and I had to make some hard choices. I studied voice and seriously considered majoring in Vocal Performance. On the other hand, Computer Science and Mathematics was attractive, too. There didn't seem to be a way to do all of them together. A Vocal major would likely end up with me in a cardboard box on Lower Wacker drive. CS/Math would provide me with food and shelter. I'm very big on food and thrive on shelter. That made my decision for me.

I've been in the software business for over 25 years, starting in C, moving to C++, then lots of Java. Now I'm looking for an opportunity to expand my horizons with full-stack web development.

When not hunched over a monitor I can be found camping in my small trailer, refereeing high school football games, going to the opera and symphony and finding the best source of dried kiwi.
